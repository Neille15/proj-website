Brgy. Marulas — Flood Plans & Activities

Valenzuela City DRRMC
📋 Flood Mitigation Plan

2024–2026 Barangay Flood Resilience Program

Brgy. Marulas has adopted a 3-year flood resilience roadmap aligned with Valenzuela City's Comprehensive Land Use Plan. Key measures include the desilting of Tullahan River tributaries, elevation of critical roads along Marulas Road, and construction of 1.2 km of concrete drainage channels co-funded by the DPWH and City Government under the Valenzuela Urban Flood Resilience Initiative.

📄 Barangay Resolution No. 2024-07March 2024
🌿 Community Activity

Annual Drainage Cleaning Drive (Brigada Kalikasan)

Every June before the monsoon season, residents join the Brigada Kalikasan — a barangay-wide drainage and creek cleaning drive. In 2024, over 400 volunteers participated, clearing 3.5 tons of solid waste from Marulas' drainage network and improving canal flow by an estimated 60%.

🌿 Brgy. Marulas Environment CommitteeJune 2024
🌊 Early Warning System

Tullahan River Flood Sensors Now Operational

The DOST-PAGASA and Valenzuela DRRMO jointly installed water level sensors along the Tullahan River near Marulas. Residents receive SMS alerts when water reaches Warning (1.5m), Critical (2.0m), and Danger (2.5m) thresholds — giving households up to 2 hours of advance warning before flooding.

🌊 Valenzuela City DRRMOAugust 2024
🚒 Training

BDRRMC Water Rescue & Life Support Training

Brgy. Marulas BDRRMC conducted a 2-day Water Rescue and Basic Life Support training for 35 barangay tanod and VAWC members in partnership with the Philippine Red Cross Valenzuela Chapter. Training covered swift water rescue, CPR, and evacuation protocols for flood scenarios.

🏥 Philippine Red Cross — ValenzuelaSeptember 2024
🏗️ Infrastructure

Box Culvert Project — Marulas Creek Improvement

A ₱8.2-million box culvert improvement project along Marulas Creek has been approved under the Valenzuela City Infrastructure Fund. The project will replace aging canal structures to handle a 25-year flood return period event and is scheduled to begin Q1 2025, expected to protect over 1,500 households.

🏗️ City Engineering Office, ValenzuelaDecember 2024
🔔 Evacuation Drill

Annual Flood Evacuation Simulation — 1,200 Participants

Brgy. Marulas conducted its annual Flood Evacuation Drill with 1,200 resident participants. The drill tested evacuation routes to Marulas Elementary School and the Barangay Hall and introduced the new color-coded flood signal system aligned with Valenzuela City's unified warning protocols.

🔔 Brgy. Marulas BDRRMCOctober 2024

🚦 Valenzuela City Flood Signal System

Yellow — Advisory

Water rising. Be alert. Prepare emergency kits. Monitor BaHALA updates.

Orange — Warning

Flooding imminent. Secure valuables. Ready to evacuate on short notice.

Red — Danger

Evacuate now. Go to nearest center: Marulas Elementary School.

Black — Extreme Danger

Life-threatening. Do not travel. Stay on highest floor. Call 911.
